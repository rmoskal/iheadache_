Your Preventative Treatment Profile

(My Profile Tab > Preventatives)

Your Preventative Treatment Profile is where you track treatments that you take to help prevent headaches (prophylaxis) rather than treatments that you take in response to an actual headache.

This part of the app works just like “acute treatment” profile in that you have common and custom tabs.  By clicking on the column heading you may sort your common and custom medications by trade (brand) name and generic name. Click the P checkmark next to preventative treatments that you want to be included in your profile. 

You are only specifying the treatments that will be included in your “preventive treatment profile”.   You will go the Preventatives tab to select start dates, stop dates, and medication dosage.

Common [Preventatives] Tab

The common tab lists about 50 of the most common treatments that have been shown to prevent headaches.  To add preventive treatments to your profile:

  • Select the checkbox next to the names of the drugs that you want to add to your profile.
  • Click the OK button to commmit all your changes. 

Note:  The presence of a medication on this list does not represent any type of endorsement by Better QOL, Inc.

Custom [Preventatives] Tab

To add a preventive treatments that’s not on our “common tab”, choose the “custom tab” and you can set up your own custom preventive treatments.

 Add a custom preventative treatment

  • click the “add” button and fill in the requested information.
  • The included column shows all the custom treatments that are in your profile so make sure that there is a checkmark by the treatment.   If at a later date you don’t want to see this treatment you can always uncheck it.
  • You can choose to enter a Trade Name and/or Generic Name.  Note:  Both fields are required in our system If you are only going to use one name then we recommend that you duplicate the name in both columns. 
  • Click the update button.  Now you will see the treatment in your custom list.
  • You can continue making any other changes to your profile but note, our changes are not applied until you press the OK button at the bottom to save the entry.

Inactivate a custom preventative medication

  • To “Inactivate” a custom medication - If you think you may want to return to this medication later, but do not want it to currently appear on your active drug list, then uncheck the “included” checkbox.
  • If you prefer to delete the medication then click on the medication you wish to delete and then press the “delete button”

Note: If you delete or inactivate a medication(s) from your profile, it will still be shown on your prior headaches.  It will just not be available for future headaches.
